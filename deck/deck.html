The problem

AI quality doesn't fail loudly. It drifts.

A retrieval index goes quietly stale. A model update shifts behavior. Every individual response still looks fine — so thresholds stay green while quality slides for days.

Your observability stack logged everything and told you nothing. You find out from customers.

And the tools that do alert? Teams mute them within weeks — because they can't tell degradation from a traffic shift.

The solution

DRIFT — the early-warning court for AI quality

production AI stream→ SENSOR · scores every response→ LEDGER · append-only, self-grading→ THE COURT · every alarm cross-examined→ COUNTDOWN · hours-to-floor + cause
LLMs argue and explain.

Deterministic code measures and extrapolates. No model ever produces a number the system acts on — regression, changepoints, confounder stats are all scipy.

Uncertainty buys evidence, not silence.

A suspicious-but-unproven trend doesn't page a human and doesn't get dismissed — WATCH tightens the sampling rate until the debate can be settled.

The system grades itself.

Every alert's outcome is backfilled when the predicted crossing does (or doesn't) happen. Alert precision is the headline metric on the dashboard.

The differentiator

No alert reaches a human until it survives cross-examination

Prosecutor

Argues the decline is real — armed with slope, R², p-value and a changepoint the code computed.

Defense

Attacks with every innocent explanation: traffic-mix shift, one outlier user, time-of-day, scorer noise, sample size.

Judge

Rules DISMISS / WATCH / ALERT on a fixed standard of proof, citing ledger rows. Judgment is auditable.
